nPSO on the hyperbolic disk

Embed nodes by popularity and similarity; search for the temperature that hits the target global clustering coefficient.

nPSO is the only generator in this repo that targets the global clustering coefficient directly. Nodes live on a hyperbolic disk: radial position by popularity (power law with exponent gamma), angular position from a mixture of c Gaussians (one per cluster). Temperature T in (0, 1) controls how strictly edges follow geometric proximity. Low T means tight clustering; high T means near-random connectivity.

The search is 1D root-finding over T. Secant step with a margin guard, bisection fallback. This walkthrough runs the procedure on the shared 20-node synthetic input at default settings, showing both the disk embedding and the (T, ccoeff) trajectory side by side.
