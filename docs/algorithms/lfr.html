The LFR benchmark generator.

Power-law fit on the input's degrees and cluster sizes, then a fresh sample through the LFR C++ binary. Walked frame by frame on the shared 20-node example.

LFR does not copy your graph. It reads the input as two distributions, fits power laws to them, and hands the fitted exponents to a C++ sampler that draws fresh values. The output's node ids, degrees, and cluster partition are a new draw, not a permutation of the input.

Default settings on our 20-node synthetic mean outlier_mode=singleton (outliers become size-1 clusters before the floor minc≥3 filters them out) and the mean-per-node μ convention for mixing. Twelve steps cover profile extraction, power-law fit, binary invocation, and output reassembly.
